A wooden pizza peel isn’t just a romantic nod to old-school pizzerias, it’s a practical tool that helps you slide a raw pie onto a blazing-hot stone with minimal sticking. Wood is naturally less conductive than metal, so it won’t shock the dough, and a well-seasoned board develops a patina that acts like a non-stick surface. But not all wood peels are created equal: the thickness of the blade, the length of the handle, and the type of wood all affect how easily you launch and retrieve your pizza. When choosing a peel, think about your oven type and the size of the pizzas you make. Most home peels measure 12 to 16 inches across, enough for a standard 12-inch pie. A thinner blade (around ⅛ inch) is better for launching, while a thicker one (¼ inch or more) helps when retrieving. Handle length matters too: a short handle gives you better control, a long one keeps your hands away from the heat. And the wood you choose, acacia, maple, bamboo, affects durability, weight, and how well it resists moisture over time.
